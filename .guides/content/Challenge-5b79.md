Calculate the second derivative for the following:

$$ k(x) = 2x^{3} - 4x^{2} + 9 $$

Teacher worked solution
$$ \begin{align}
	   k'(x) &= 2(3x^{2}) - 4(2x) + 0 \\
	   &= 6x^{2} - 8x \\
	    &   \\
	    k''(x) &= 6(2x) - 8  \\
	    &=  12x - 8
	    \end{align} $$
